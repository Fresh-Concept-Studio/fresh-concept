Life is motion, but our knowledge of biology has largely been informed by static snapshots. We're changing that.

Our Core Motivation

Eikon's core driver is to bring us closer to the true mechanisms underlying biology and pharmacology to apply novel approaches to drug discovery. Until recently, it was not possible to observe protein movement in living cells. Direct visualization of individual proteins moving in living cells is a window to biological ground truth. Eikon is leveraging this new access, and the disruptive new understandings of biology that result, to identify therapies in a manner not previously possible.

The Technology

Our therapeutic discovery platform is rooted in a Nobel prize-winning technology, super-resolved fluorescence microscopy, invented by our founder, physicist Eric Betzig (Chemistry, 2014). This advance made it possible to observe individual proteins in a living cell, where previously only cellular organelles could be directly visualized. Eric collaborated with Eikon's other founders, Xavier Darzacq, Luke Lavis and Robert Tjian, to enable tracking the dynamic movement of specific, known individual proteins in living cells. What they observed transformed long-held assumptions about biology and demonstrated the importance of this visualization to true biological understanding.

The Team

Eikon was founded to develop therapies based on this novel ability to track the movement of specific proteins in living cells. We have amassed a team with expertise across biology, chemistry, physics, microscopy, engineering, automation and computing to create a high-throughput screening platform to detect compound-induced changes in protein dynamics. With our internally-developed platform and process, we can label and screen essentially any protein in the proteome, creating the opportunity to pursue therapeutic targets based on profiling their dynamics. With a fundamentally novel mechanism and approach to therapeutic discovery, Eikon will identify and develop therapeutics that impact a wide range of diseases with unmet medical needs.
